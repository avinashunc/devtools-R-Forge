    <article class="markdown-body entry-content" itemprop="mainContentOfPage"><h1>
<a id="user-content-metapopplan" class="anchor" href="#metapopplan" aria-hidden="true"><span class="octicon octicon-link"></span></a>METAPOPPLAN</h1>

<p>an R package for spatial conservation planning in Growling Grass Frog metapopulations</p>

<ul class="task-list">
<li>The purpose of METAPOPPLAN is to provide a framework for choosing optimal sets of wetlands to retain or construct, so as to minimise extinction risk for Growling Grass Frog <em>(Litoria raniformis)</em> metapopulations.</li>
<li>The risk-assessment methodology is based on the analytic approximation of metapopulation extinction risk derived by Frank and Wissel (2002), but with empirically-derived estimates of extinction and colonisation parameters substituted for the theoretical values used by Frank and Wissel.</li>
<li>The empirical extinction and colonisation parameters are taken from the model of Heard et al. (2013). The posterior means of the MCMC samples from the model's parameters are used (i.e. parametric uncertainty is ignored).</li>
<li>Simulated annealing is used to choose efficient combinations of wetlands for creation or retention, subject to an overall budget and the costs of creation or retention ascribed to each individual wetland under consideration.</li>
<li>Requires the related package <a href="https://github.com/scrogster/METAPOP">METAPOP</a>, which provides facilities for stochastic simulation of Growling Grass Frog metapopulations. </li>
</ul>

</code></pre>
