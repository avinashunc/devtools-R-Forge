    <article class="markdown-body entry-content" itemprop="mainContentOfPage"><h1>
<a id="user-content-rydn" class="anchor" href="#rydn" aria-hidden="true"><span class="octicon octicon-link"></span></a>rydn</h1>

<p>R interface to the Yahoo! Developers Network APIs.</p>

<p>Currently focused on the <a href="https://developer.yahoo.com/boss/">BOSS APIs</a> to do geolocation. At the moment, the following APIs are supported:</p>

<ul class="task-list">
<li><a href="https://developer.yahoo.com/boss/placefinder/">PlaceFinder</a></li>
</ul>

<h2>
<a id="user-content-api-key" class="anchor" href="#api-key" aria-hidden="true"><span class="octicon octicon-link"></span></a>API Key</h2>

<p>In order to use this package, you'll need to register an application with Yahoo!. You can do that <a href="https://developer.apps.yahoo.com/dashboard/createKey.html?api=boss">here</a>.</p>

<h2>
<a id="user-content-pricing" class="anchor" href="#pricing" aria-hidden="true"><span class="octicon octicon-link"></span></a>Pricing</h2>

<p>The Yahoo! BOSS APIs are non-open, commercial APIs that have charges associated with them. You can find more information about pricing <a href="https://info.yahoo.com/legal/us/yahoo/boss/pricing/">here</a>. There is also a non-commercial mode (<code>commercial=FALSE</code>) in which <code>rydn</code> will use the non-commercial YQL API which, at the time of writing, <a href="https://developer.yahoo.com/boss/geo/docs/free_YQL.html">is free</a>.</p>

<h2>
<a id="user-content-faq" class="anchor" href="#faq" aria-hidden="true"><span class="octicon octicon-link"></span></a>FAQ</h2>

<p><strong>Why another Geocoder for R?</strong></p>

<p>TL;DR: Every other API I looked at either couldn't geocode intersections properly, had overall low quality on the samples I tried, and/or required that I display the results on their branded map. Every commercial option I looked at was cost-prohibitive (thousands of dollars per year to start). Yahoo! BOSS offered a more reasonable price structure and seems to have high quality; it also offers a free mode currently for under 2,000 queries a day. (Be sure to set <code>commercial=FALSE</code> in your requests.)</p>

<p>It turns out that most freely available Geocoders have pretty stringent limitations on what you're allowed to do with their data. Below are a few options and why they didn't work for particular applications of mine. (You should check for yourself, as these restrictions/problems may have changed since I last looked at the service, or I may have misunderstood them.)</p>

<ul class="task-list">
<li>
<a href="http://developer.mapquest.com/web/products/open/geocoding-service"><strong>MapQuest Open</strong></a> - Mapquest provides an API around Open Street Map data that can do geocoding, but it's featureset and accuracy is somewhat limited. It also can't do cross-street geocoding ("Main St and Harper Ave").</li>
<li>
<a href="http://developer.mapquest.com/web/products/open/geocoding-service"><strong>MapQuest Commercial</strong></a> - A commercially licensed geocoder that is more accurate and does cross-street geocoding. If memory serves, they'll give you a limited number of queries on their Commercial geocoder for free, but the service is geared towards customers entering their data into your service directly, and you're required to show the results on a MapQuest map, which you might not want to do. Purchasing access to the commercial license was cost-prohibitive for me. From <a href="http://developer.mapquest.com/web/tools/getting-started/platform/dev-faq">their FAQ</a>:</li>
</ul>

<blockquote>
<p>Geocoding usage in the Community Edition on Licensed Data is limited to only the locations entered by an end user or that you have uploaded through our Data Manager tool. Geocodes under the Community Edition on Licensed Data cannot be stored for use outside the MapQuest service. If you would like more flexible options for geocode use, please contact MapQuest Platform Services to learn more about our extended rights geocoding product. Geocoding on Open Data has different terms, please check the Terms of Use for details.</p>
</blockquote>

<ul class="task-list">
<li><p><a href="https://developers.google.com/maps/documentation/geocoding/"><strong>Google</strong></a> - A great geocoder with serious restrictions. "The Geocoding API may only be used in conjunction with a Google map; geocoding results without displaying them on a map is prohibited." Commercial licensing was cost-prohibitive for me.</p></li>
<li><p><a href="https://developer.here.com/"><strong>Nokia HERE</strong></a> - Same <a href="https://developer.here.com/terms-conditions-base">restriction</a> about showing the geocoded results on one of their branded maps. "You may not use Results (i) without a HERE map or underlying imagery with exception of standalone usage for directions, places, geocoding and traffic, or (ii) in connection with a third party map, street level or satellite imagery."</p></li>
</ul>
